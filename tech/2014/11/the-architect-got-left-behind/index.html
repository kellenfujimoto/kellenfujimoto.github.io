The Architect Got Left Behind

Wed, Nov 5, 2014 ~200 words

Way back when I was in high school, all of my schoolwork fell into a similar pattern: I would be either monumentally enthusiastic or apprehensive towards every assignment. I’d dive head first into a project, or freeze at the sight of 80 whole pages of reading a night.1

I was fortunate enough to attend a school that encourages personal growth and self reflection, and so I learned to structure my work and channel my enthusiasm into productivity. It is a continuing process, but I like to think that I correct myself earlier as I practice more.

This diversion is an explanation. Though I am extremely excited to get Tsunami off the ground, I jumped into prototyping without considering the structure of the app. In plowing forward along the critical path, I boxed myself into a monolithic app which would make future improvements difficult at best.

Keeping this in mind, I’m going to be blogging more about the structure of Tsunami, and developing a set of meta-goals for the project to guide future development. Expect more soon!
